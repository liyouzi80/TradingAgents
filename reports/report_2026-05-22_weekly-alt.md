## 4. Important Structural Considerations for USO

### 4.1 Contango / Backwardation Risk
USO rolls futures contracts monthly. In **contango** (futures prices > spot prices), rolling contracts creates a "roll cost" that can cause the ETF to underperform the spot price of oil over time. Conversely, **backwardation** (spot > futures) benefits the fund.

### 4.2 K-1 Tax Treatment
USO is structured as a Limited Partnership. Investors typically receive a **Schedule K-1** rather than a 1099 for tax purposes, which may have implications for tax filing.

### 4.3 Correlation to Crude Oil
USO tracks near-month WTI futures. Due to the roll mechanism, long-term holding may diverge significantly from the spot price of oil. It is best suited for **short-to-medium-term tactical positions** rather than long-term buy-and-hold.
